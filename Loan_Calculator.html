🏦 Loan Repayment Calculator
📖 How to use this calculator
Basic Parameters
Loan Amount — The original principal you borrowed (or remaining balance if mid-loan).
Annual Interest Rate — Nominal annual rate. The calculator shows the effective annual rate (EAR) and monthly rate below the slider.
Loan Duration — Enter years + months for exact remaining term. Example: 3 yrs + 7 mo = 43 months left.
Early Repayment
Extra Amount — One-time lump sum paid directly to principal each time.
Every X months — How often you make the extra payment. Set to 1 for monthly, 12 for annual, etc.
Start at Month — Delay the first prepayment if needed (e.g. month 13 = start after first year).
The savings banner shows total interest saved and how many months earlier you pay off the loan.
Refinancing
Refinance at Year — When you switch to a new loan with a lower rate.
New Rate — The rate of the refinanced loan. The remaining balance is recalculated at this rate.
Refinancing Cost — One-time fee (bank charges, notary, etc.) added to the balance. The banner tells you if refinancing is net-positive after this cost.
You can combine early repayment + refinancing to model the optimal payoff strategy.
Charts & Tabs
Balance Over Time — Remaining debt each month. Compare original vs. your strategy.
Interest vs Principal — Donut breakdown of total cost + cumulative interest curve.
Annual Breakdown — Stacked bar: how much went to principal vs interest each year.
Amortization Table — Year-by-year detail. 🟢 Green = prepayment year, 🟠 Orange = refinancing event.
⚠️ This calculator assumes a standard annuity loan (equal monthly payments). Results are estimates — consult your bank for exact figures. EAR = Effective Annual Rate.
