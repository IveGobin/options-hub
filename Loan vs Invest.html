⚖️ Loan vs. Invest — Decision Analyzer
Is it better to pay off your loan or invest? Enter your parameters and get a clear answer.
📖 How to use this tool
Loan (left panel) Enter loan amount, interest rate and duration. Free cash is the key parameter — how much you have extra each month (above your regular payment) to either prepay or invest. This amount is identical in both scenarios; only its use differs.
Investment (right panel) Set the expected annual return (S&P 500 historical avg ~8-10%). Break-even rate shows the return at which both options are equal — below that rate, paying off the loan wins; above it, investing wins.
Net worth comparison Scenario A: free cash goes toward early loan repayment; after the loan is paid off, that same amount goes into investments. Scenario B: pay the minimum installment only and invest free cash immediately alongside the loan.
Psychological factor This tool is purely mathematical — it does not account for the stress of carrying debt, the emotional value of being debt-free, or the risk of investment volatility. Conservative investors often prefer the certainty of debt repayment.
⚠️ Educational tool. Does not account for tax relief, bank fees, inflation (unless enabled), or rate changes. Consult a financial advisor.
